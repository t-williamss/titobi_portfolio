TREE Lab Website

  • Overview

    The Technology, Race, Ethics and Equity (TREE) Lab began in 2019 and I was commisioned to design and build its lab website. This website was designed to be a publicity and recruitment tool for the lab in order to attract potential student researchers and community partners for collaborations.

    The goal of this particular project was to build a fabulous website that spotlights the research lab as a whole and the work the team accomplishes.

Background

The TREE Lab is a group of researchers interested in advancing technolgoy while socially critquing it and I wanted to design a wesbite that not only reflected our research, but also the community we were seeking to build.

I was the sole designer and developer of this project.

Defining the Problem Statement

Motivation

A challenge over the course of this project was focusing on key uses of the lab website as it can be a hub for the student researchers, area to host projects, publicise lab activities, recruit student and cokmmunity partners, and more. Research using other lab websites and user opinion concluded the TREE Lab website is best suited as a publicity and recruitment tool.

User Personas

Based on my research, I recognized that there were 2 key user types that the TREE Lab website was applicable.

Ideating & Conceptualization

Brainstorming

I brainstormed through looking at university research lab websites and understanding their general format included the following pages: people, projects, contact information, and about. With this information, I designed pages for each of these categories and specifically for the TREE Lab's needs, a page to spotlight its community partners.

Software Development

To develop the product, I used HTML, CSS/SASS, JavaScript, and the framework Jekyll to build the TREE Lab Website. This project has taught me best practices for coding as the website I have built will be used beyond my time as a university student and needs to be clear and concose for future developers.

Results & Reflection

This project was instrumental in developing my design and front-end engineering skills as I presented my designs, sought feedback from users, and built the website.

Some key takeaways from this project are:

  • Iterative Process of Development. Throughout building this website, the design changed numerous times as well as the tools used to build it. The iterative process used to build on code I found interesting and a welcome departure from the straightforward school assignment process.

  • Focus on the user. Earlier in my journey, I made the mistake of worrying about the look of the UI before deciding who were the users of the TREE Lab website which led to future complications that could have been avoided. Taking a step back and reassessing the user flows helped me to reprioritise the users and UX.

  • Be open. At the beginning of this project, I had no interest in front-end engineering and design , but over the course of this project I have surpised myself and enjoyed every moment. It serves as a reminder to always be open to exploring and trying new things.
